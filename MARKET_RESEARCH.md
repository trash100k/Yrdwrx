# YardWorx — US Market-Entry & Product-Gap Research

_Deep-research synthesis (US national landscaping-software market). 28 sources fetched, 25 claims
adversarially verified (23 confirmed / 2 refuted). Generated 2026-06-27. Verify time-sensitive
pricing before any GTM decision._

## Bottom line

YardWorx is entering a **mature, feature-commoditized** market. The features its own notes flag as
gaps — QuickBooks, online payments, recurring billing — are the **entry bar**, shipped by every
incumbent, not differentiators. Its two flagship AI bets are **no longer greenfield** (Jobber ships
a voice assistant; Aspire/SatQuote/SiteRecon ship AI estimating). But one lane is open and
defensible: **live, on-site, customer-facing visual selling** — co-creating a designed, priced,
good/better/best proposal in the customer's driveway — because incumbents all anchor on *remote
aerial measurement to avoid site visits*, the opposite workflow.

## 1. Feature gaps = launch blockers (not differentiators)

Verified 3-0. Table-stakes are shipped by all majors; YardWorx's gap list maps almost 1:1.

- **Jobber**: unified quote→schedule→invoice→get-paid→message; online card/debit/**ACH**; two-way
  SMS; one-way **QuickBooks** sync. [1][2][3]
- **LMN**: QuickBooks (QBO + Desktop), customer portal, route optimization, GPS, real-time job
  costing, LMN Pay. [12]
- **SingleOps**: **two-way** QuickBooks (Online + Desktop) + integrated card/ACH that auto-syncs
  back. [14]
- Industry tool usage: **accounting 77%, invoicing 72%, estimates 61%** — accounting is not optional.

**QuickBooks is a genuine moat** (3-0): every benchmarked vendor treats it as core ("double entry is
a thing of the past"); depth ranges one-way (Jobber) → two-way (SingleOps, LMN Desktop).

> **Must-fix before paid launch:** QuickBooks sync, online card/ACH payments, recurring/seasonal
> billing, online booking / instant quote, time-tracking → payroll.

## 2. AI differentiation — contested, but one lane open

Verified 3-0:
- **Jobber Voice** (Sept 2025, GA at launch, 100+ tasks: quotes, invoices, client updates) directly
  overlaps **"Live Ear."** Voice-admin lead is largely neutralized. [21][22]
- **Aspire PropertyIntel**: AI-assisted measurement + auto-calc of time/material/cost **from the
  contractor's own pricing DB** — the same "grounded in your catalog" prop YardWorx claims. [5][6]
- **SatQuote / SiteRecon**: aerial estimating — SiteRecon case studies cite **24-hour proposals (vs
  3–4 days), +300 hrs/yr, doubled close rates.** [19][20]
- **ReimagineHome.ai** already ships consumer photo-to-design; **ServiceTitan Atlas** is a native AI
  sidekick.

**The open lane** (verified 3-0): incumbents anchor on *remote aerial measurement to avoid site
visits*. YardWorx's edge is the opposite — **live, on-site, customer-facing visual selling**. Frame
it as **"on-site visual selling / closing,"** NOT "AI estimating" (aerial owns it) or "voice admin"
(Jobber owns it).

> **Lead with:** (1) on-site photo-to-vision + tiered quote (Design Studio, made reliable);
> (2) live voice quoting in front of the customer (Live Ear repositioned as a *closing* tool);
> (3) **add or partner for aerial/satellite takeoff** (Nearmap / SatQuote-style) to neutralize the
> remote-measurement speed gap.

## 3. Pricing & packaging

SMB band is **$200–$650/mo**; Aspire deliberately abandons small operators.

| Tool | Pricing | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **LMN** | Starter **$297/mo** (+ ~$797 onboarding); Pro **$648/mo** (+ ~$1,497 onboarding) | Mandatory setup fee = friction to undercut [9][10][11] |
| **SingleOps** | $220 / $385 / $550 /mo | + per-extra-user; 9% annual discount [13] |
| **Jobber** | Core ~$39 / Connect ~$119 / Grow ~$199 / Plus ~$599 | per-user caps, ~35% annual discount |
| **Yardbook** | Free + ~$35 / ~$50 /mo | anchors the floor [15] |
| **Aspire** | Revenue-tiered ($1M–3M / $3M–15M / $15M+), **unlimited users** | explicitly **not** for startups [7][8] |

> **Suggested structure:** **no onboarding fee** (undercut LMN), a **free/low entry tier** to land,
> then Pro (~$199–299/mo: AI selling suite + QuickBooks + payments) and Enterprise. Land-and-expand
> on the AI upsell tool. Avoid Aspire's revenue-tier model — their turf.

## 4. Positioning & recommended beachhead

**Beachhead: small-to-mid residential design-build / installation landscapers.**
- They live on **visual selling + good/better/best upsell** — your strength.
- They sit **below Aspire's revenue floor** and are under-served at the SMB price point.
- Commercial maintenance is **Aspire's owned turf** (heavier on routing/scale you lack).
- US landscaping ≈ **$186B (2025) → $256B by 2031** (5.46% CAGR); maintenance ≈ 44% of revenue.

**Secondary wedge:** pesticide-applying **lawn-care** operators — federal rule still requires
commercial applicators to furnish data to the customer **within 30 days** of a **restricted-use**
application; YardWorx's EPA-logging + signature + audit-trail + portal can fulfill it. _(Medium
confidence, 2-1. Restricted-use only; 23 states add rules; a "14-day schema" claim was refuted —
don't over-specify compliance in marketing without legal review.)_

> **Positioning statement:** _"Close the job in the driveway. YardWorx turns a phone photo and a
> conversation into a designed, priced, good/better/best proposal your customer signs on the spot —
> then syncs to QuickBooks and gets you paid."_

## Refuted claims (killed in verification — do not repeat)

- ❌ "Yardbook is free, monetized via sponsored ads rather than subscriptions." (1-2)
- ❌ "Each restricted-use application must be recorded within 14 days with a specific fixed schema."
  (0-3) — do not state exact pesticide recordkeeping schemas without state-by-state legal review.

## Open questions worth answering before building more

1. Is **Design Studio reliable enough to win a live head-to-head demo** vs PropertyIntel/SatMeasure?
   The whole differentiation rests on the on-site demo landing — and internal notes flag reliability
   issues. → Fix Design Studio reliability early.
2. Actual US market size / willingness-to-pay of **design-build vs recurring-maintenance** segments,
   and which has lower churn / higher AI-upsell attach.
3. **Build vs partner** for aerial/satellite measurement (Nearmap / SatQuote), and cost vs perfecting
   photo-based quoting.
4. QuickBooks: **one-way (faster) vs two-way (stickier)** for launch, given the Supabase migration.
5. What actually drives churn — does a "no onboarding fee, free tier" wedge beat LMN's setup fee, or
   do switching costs (QuickBooks history, crew retraining) dominate?
